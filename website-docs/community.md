# Community rules

Four roles (Consumer, Contributor, Reviewer, Supervisor), contribution and review flow, reporting and arbitration.

## 1. What you can do

The homepage offers four entry points for four ways to participate (one account can do multiple):

- **Find information** — Browse the catalog, search by category or keyword for tools, data sources, skills, and agents; view entry details; rate or comment on entries you use.
- **Create content** — Submit new tools/services, skills, or agents, or submit updates to existing entries.
- **Participate in review** — Handle pending contributions (approve, reject, or request changes) and user reports or appeals.
- **Report issues** — When you find problematic content, unfair review, or suspect fake reviews, submit a report for platform reviewers to handle.

## 2. How to contribute

Submit entries or updates under «Create content». You can submit:

### New tool or service

You found an API, tool, or data source useful for agents and want to add it to the catalog.

*Example:* Submit name, link, category, description, and tags.

### New skill or agent

You want to register a skill or your own agent in the catalog for others to discover.

*Example:* Submit capability description, doc link, or usage.

### Update existing entry

An entry is outdated or wrong; you can submit a correction or update.

*Example:* Under «Create content» choose update, link the entry, and fill in the new content.

After submission it goes to pending review. When approved, the entry appears in the catalog with a unique ID. If rejected or changes are requested, you see the reason or suggestions and can resubmit or appeal.

## 3. How to review

If you have reviewer access, you can see pending contributions and reports under «Participate in review». For contributions you can approve (entry goes public), reject (with reason), or request changes (with suggestions). For reports and appeals you decide whether they are upheld and take action (e.g. delist, close unfounded reports). Your decisions are recorded for audit.

## 4. How to report

When you find false, infringing, or gamed content, or clearly unfair review, submit a report under «Report issues». Include the target (which entry or review), reason, and evidence if any. Reports are queued for reviewers; you can check status under «My reports». Do not abuse reporting; misuse may affect your account.

## 5. Conventions to know

- Contributions, reports, feedback, review, and arbitration are write operations and require authentication; no anonymous writes.
- Catalog content is reviewed; unapproved submissions do not appear.
- Each public entry has a unique ID that does not change, for precise reference and sharing.
- Same-case role recusal: contributors cannot review their own; reviewers who handled earlier steps cannot do final arbitration.
- False content, fake reviews, or violations are handled (delist, demote, etc.); repeated violations may affect account.
- If you disagree with a decision, you can appeal or report again for another reviewer to review.
